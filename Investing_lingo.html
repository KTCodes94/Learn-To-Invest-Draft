Common Investment Lingo:

This Lesson talks about common investment lingo for beginners

Market

A place where buyers and sellers get together to make transactions (physical place or an electronic gathering spot like NASDAQ)

Bullish

refers to the direction of the market going up

Bearish

refers to the direction of the market going down

Asset Classes

Different types of Investments

Equities

typically refers to owning shares in a company such as stocks, mutual funds and ETFs

Stocks

shares of ownerships in a company

Mutual Funds

pool of investment dollars that can often be professionally managed. Having a mutual fund usually means you have stakes in multiple companies

ETFs

similar to Mutual Funds but can be bought and sold on the same trading day

Fixed Income

Includes Bonds Treasuries and Certificates of Deposits (CDs). Payments are fixed. They are consistent and delivered in regular intervals

Derivatives

Security with price dependent on another asset. Includes Options, Futures, Currencies. Commonly tracks things like a stock index where the value is derived. Uses leverage which controls assetss with a small amount of money.

Cash

savings and money market accounts. High liquidity and low risk.

Portfolio

combined asset combination

Allocation

percentage of portfolio in each asset class. This might change over time depending on your risk tolerance level or things like approaching

Diversification

having varied types of individual assets within an asset class.
